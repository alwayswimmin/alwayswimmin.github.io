Money in Pride

Jun 27, 2017

As part of the Resist Movement, someone in the second group of the SF Pride Parade held up a sign reading "Get cops and corporations out of our parade." Initially, I felt apprehensive about this sentiment; I knew a good number of interns marching in the Facebook contingent, and I had trouble reasoning why allies with good intent ought not march.

I still feel that we are moving in the right direction when police officers, politicians, and other members of government demonstrate their solidarity with the pride movement. But towards the end of the parade, the corparate presence in particular was very noticable. Most corporations were fairly innocuous, simply passing out rainbow stickers, but some companies were advertising quite blatantly: my friends and I were collecting Clif bars and Chipotle buy-one-get-one-free coupons. Is that what we came out to the parade for? Corporate representation at pride should be in support of employees and the community at large, not for a business promotion.
